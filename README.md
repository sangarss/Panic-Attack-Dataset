Panic Attack Dataset Analysis
Project Overview

This project analyzes a Panic Attack Dataset to identify patterns related to triggers, symptoms, lifestyle habits, and demographic factors that may influence panic attacks. The analysis focuses on understanding how factors such as sleep duration, caffeine intake, alcohol consumption, psychological triggers, and medical history relate to panic attack frequency and severity.

Interactive dashboards were created to visualize relationships between panic scores, attack frequency, sleep patterns, and symptoms, providing insights that may help in understanding behavioral and physiological indicators associated with panic attacks.

Dataset Description

The dataset contains records of individuals experiencing panic attacks along with multiple related attributes, including:

Demographics (age group, gender)

Panic score levels (Low, Medium, High)

Sleep duration

Panic attack duration

Weekly alcohol consumption

Trigger factors (Caffeine, Phobia, PTSD, Social Anxiety)

Medical history (Anxiety, Depression, None)

Physical symptoms (Dizziness, Trembling, Chest Pain, Sweating, Shortness of Breath)

This dataset can be used for data analysis, visualization, and predictive modeling related to mental health patterns.

Key Observations

Certain triggers such as PTSD and Social Anxiety show higher panic scores compared to others.

Sleep duration patterns appear related to panic attack occurrence.

Symptoms like sweating and shortness of breath are among the most frequently reported.

Panic attack duration varies widely across individuals.
